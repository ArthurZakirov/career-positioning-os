# Reference Material

These are optional external resources to use when iterating on resumes, LinkedIn sections, article positioning, and achievement representation.

Do not copy proprietary text from these resources into this repo. Use them as thinking inputs for structure, evaluation criteria, and persuasion mechanics.

## Software Engineering Career Positioning

### The Google Resume

- Link: https://www.amazon.de/The-Google-Resume/dp/8126538058
- Book: *The Google Resume* by Gayle Laakmann McDowell.
- Use for: software-engineering resume strategy, technical career positioning, achievement selection, and how top technology companies evaluate candidates.

When using this with an AI agent, provide only the specific notes, principles, or excerpts you are allowed to use. Ask the agent to apply the principle to your own achievement packet instead of copying phrasing.

### A Life Engineered

- Link: https://www.youtube.com/@ALifeEngineered/videos
- Use for: software-engineering career strategy, resume iteration, job-search tactics, and practical framing for technical candidates.

When iterating on resume bullets or LinkedIn Experience sections, use this as a software-engineering-specific calibration source: does the artifact read like credible engineering impact, or like generic business copy?

## Offer And Value Psychology

### Acquisition.com Offers Course / $100M Offers

- Link: https://www.acquisition.com/training/offers
- Use for: understanding how people perceive value, why a promise feels compelling, how to make the desired outcome concrete, and how to reduce perceived risk.

This is not software-engineering-specific, but the mental model transfers. Treat a resume bullet, LinkedIn bio, article, or profile as an "offer": the reader invests time and attention expecting a desired positive outcome.

Apply the offer lens to career artifacts:

- Who is the reader?
- What outcome do they want?
- What problem or risk do they want removed?
- What proof makes the claim believable?
- What mechanism explains why the result happened?
- What makes the next step feel worth the reader's time?
